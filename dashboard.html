🏆 Conditions for Passing the Truck Driver Program Exam

Fastway Truck & Forklift Driving School

To pass your road test, you must operate your vehicle safely, and:

  • Daily Inspection Test: Maximum 1 error allowed
  • Uncoupling & Coupling: Up to 2 errors total, no more than 5 pull-ups
  • On-road & Backing Skills Test: Up to 30 errors allowed, with backing completed within 10 minutes

📊 Air Pressure Specifications

  • Normal Operating Pressure: 100-120 PSI
  • Low-air Warning Activation: 55 PSI
  • Compressor Cut-in & Cut-out Range: 80 - 145 PSI

1️⃣ Air Brake System

1.1 - Slow Air Pressure Build-Up Rate

Definition: The vehicle has a minor defect when it takes longer than two minutes for air pressure to build up from 85 to 100 psi.

Procedure:

  1. START THE TRUCK
  2. Check the air gauges and say air pressure is more than 100 PSI (normal operating range: 100-120 PSI)
  3. Pump the service brake pedal to drop the Air Pressure to 80 PSI
  4. Start the timer from 85 PSI and keep RPM between 600 to 900
  5. Once air pressure reaches 100 PSI, stop the timer
  6. Report: "It took (___) seconds to build air pressure from 85 PSI to 100 PSI, which is less than 2 minutes"
  7. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

1.2 - Audible Air Leaks & Air Loss

Definition: Minor defect if air leak heard; Major defect if air-loss rate exceeds limit (pressure drops more than 4 psi in one minute). Note: For Class D, specification is 3 PSI.

Procedure:

  1. Check air gauges - pressure should be above 100 PSI
  2. Release both brakes (YELLOW IN & RED IN)
  3. Press service brakes and wait for few seconds
  4. Report exact Air Pressure (___) PSI
  5. Turn off tractor, then turn Ignition on
  6. Roll down window, start timer, and check for air leakage
  7. Keep holding service brake pedal for 1 minute
  8. Report: "Air loss is [amount] psi. It's less than 4 psi"
  9. Apply both parking brakes
  10. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

1.3 - Pushrod Stroke Adjustment Limit

Definition: Vehicle has a defect when pushrod stroke exceeds the prescribed limit.

Procedure:

  1. Start engine and ensure pressure is 100 psi or above
  2. Release tractor parking brakes
  3. Move steering all way to the left
  4. Open the hood and turn off engine (keep ignition on)
  5. Go outside, measure chamber size, and mark the pushrod
  6. Go inside cab and apply service brake with brake buddy (pressure 90-100 psi)
  7. Go outside again and measure pushrod stroke
  8. Report: "Brake chamber size is 24. Maximum adjustment limit is 45 mm. Stroke is within allowed limit."
  9. Read Schedule 1 & (YELLOW OUT)
Standard Brake Chamber Size: 24
Maximum Adjustment Limit: 45 mm

1.4 - Inoperative Towing Vehicle Tractor Protection Valve

Note: Not applicable for Class D

Procedure:

  1. Start the truck
  2. Go outside and disconnect the service line (BLUE) - place on bobcat grill
  3. Come inside and confirm air pressure is above 100 PSI
  4. Release tractor brake (YELLOW IN)
  5. After few seconds, report air pressure (___) PSI
  6. Turn off truck, turn Ignition on, press brake pedal and wait 10 seconds
  7. After 10-15 seconds, confirm no audible air leak from service line
  8. Report: "Tractor protection system is working"
  9. Pull YELLOW (OUT) and reconnect service line
  10. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

1.5 - Low-Air Warning System

Definition: Major defect when low air warning fails to activate or activates before air pressure drops below 55 psi.

Procedure:

  1. Start the truck
  2. Ensure air pressure is 100 or above PSI
  3. Pump the brake pedal and drop air pressure until buzzer activates
  4. Once buzzer is ON, report: "Low air warning activated at (___) PSI, which is above 55 PSI. Everything is good."
  5. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

1.6 - Hydraulic Brakes

Note: This vehicle is equipped with air brakes and does not have a hydraulic brake system.

If Hydraulic Brakes Present:

  • Check brake-fluid level - must be above minimum
  • Ensure not less than one-quarter full
  • Check for leaks
  • Verify no cracks or damage
  • Confirm properly attached
  • Read Schedule 1

2️⃣ Cab

3️⃣ Coupling Devices

3.1 - Check Coupler Mounting & Fastener

Inspection Steps:

  1. Fifth wheel is properly attached with frame using Coupler and Fasteners
  2. No crack or damage on fifth wheel and mounting plate
  3. All Couplers and fasteners are present, tight and secure
  4. No loose or missing fasteners
  5. Safety PIN is present
  6. Check other side - same verification
  7. Fifth wheel on opposite side is properly attached
  8. All Couplers and fasteners present, tight and secure
  9. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

3.2 - Check Coupling Security

Definition: Major defect if movement exceeds 1/2 inch (13 mm) in forward/rearward direction or if coupler is damaged, broken, cracked, or has loose/missing components.

Procedure:

  1. Fifth wheel release handle is in locked position
  2. All fasteners on coupler are tight and secure (check both sides)
  3. Fifth wheel and apron plate have no crack, damage, or gap
  4. Go under trailer and check that Jaws are locked (coupler is secure)
  5. Go inside, start truck, release truck brake (YELLOW IN)
  6. Put transmission in Drive and move forward
  7. Pull tractor brake immediately (YELLOW OUT), put transmission in Neutral
  8. Come outside and mark the line on upper coupler plate front of safety pin
  9. Go back inside, release truck brake (YELLOW IN)
  10. Put transmission in Reverse and stretch truck backward
  11. Pull tractor brake (YELLOW OUT), put transmission in Neutral
  12. Come outside and mark the line again on upper coupler plate
  13. Measure both points
  14. Report: "Marks are in same place - there is no free play. Coupling is secure."
  15. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

3.3 - Check Coupler Locking Mechanism

Definition: Defect when coupling or locking mechanism is damaged or fails to lock.

Procedure:

  1. Check fifth wheel release handle is in locked position (IN)
  2. All fasteners on mounting plate are present, tight and secure
  3. Fifth wheel and upper coupler plate have no crack, damage, or gap
  4. Go under trailer and verify Jaw is locked around kingpin
  5. No crack, damage, or gap between fifth wheel and trailer plate
  6. Check other side - same verification
  7. Go inside, start truck, release truck brake (YELLOW IN)
  8. Put transmission in Drive and stretch truck 2 times (Tug Test)
  9. Apply tractor brake (YELLOW OUT) and come outside
  10. Verify fifth wheel release handle is IN
  11. Verify no gap between fifth wheel and apron plate
  12. Verify Jaw is still locked
  13. Report: "Coupler locking mechanism is working properly"
  14. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

4️⃣ Exhaust System

4.1 - Exhaust System Inspection

Procedure:

  1. Start the truck and open the hood
  2. Check complete exhaust system for cracks, damage, leaks, or smell
  3. Inspect Muffler:
    • No crack or damage
    • Properly attached to tractor body
    • No exhaust leak or smell
  4. Get in cab and run cab fan
  5. Check for any leaks or smell
  6. Report: "There is no exhaust leak in the cab"
  7. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

5️⃣ Frame & Cargo Body

5.1 - Frame and Cargo Body Inspection

FOR FRAME:

  • Open the hood
  • Check frame from front to back for cracks, damages, or bends
  • Frame members are tight and secure
  • No crack or damage
  • No sagging, collapsing or visible shifting

FOR CARGO BODY:

  • Check trailer from front to back, left to right for damages
  • Reflectors are present
  • Safety sticker is present
  • Landing legs have no crack or damage
  • Check under trailer for sagging or shifting on cross members
  • Cross members properly attached
  • Check boggy for cracks or damages
  • Lock pins are present and in locked position
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

6️⃣ Fuel System

6.1 - Fuel System Inspection

Inspection Steps:

  1. Fuel tank cap:
    • Present and tight and secure
    • No crack or damage
  2. Fuel tank straps:
    • Tight and properly attached
    • Free from cracks, rust, or damage
    • Securely hold tank at all times
  3. Fuel tank body:
    • No crack or damage on tank
    • Properly mounted to vehicle frame
    • Not loose or shifting
  4. Look under tank for fuel leaks on floor
  5. Check under engine for leaks under hood
  6. Check second fuel tank (if equipped)
  7. Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

7️⃣ Suspension System

7.1 - Check Air Suspension

Inspection Steps:

  • Check airbag for:
    • No cuts, damage, patches, or bruises
    • Properly mounted
    • Properly inflated
    • No air leak
  • Read Schedule for minor defects (air leak in air suspension system)
  • Read Schedule for major defects (damaged, cut, bruised, cracked to braid, deflated airbag, or insecurely mounted airbag)

7.2 - Check Spring Leaf

Inspection Steps:

  • Check both spring leaves for:
    • No cracks or damage
    • Not shifted
    • Not touching any component
    • Properly mounted
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

7.3 - Check Suspension Fastener

Inspection Steps:

  • Both U-bolts:
    • Present, no crack or damage
    • Tight and secure
  • Shock absorber fasteners:
    • Present, no crack or damage
    • Tight and secure
  • Leaf spring hanger fasteners:
    • Present, no crack or damage
    • Tight and secure
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

8️⃣ Tires

8.1 - Check for Tire Leak

Inspection Steps:

  1. Hit the tire with a hammer and check tire pressure (tire not deflated)
  2. No air leak heard or felt from outer side wall
  3. No air leak heard or felt from tread side
  4. No air leak heard or felt from inner side wall
  5. No air leak heard or felt from air valve
  6. Tire is not touching any other component
  7. If rear tires:
    • Tires not touching each other
    • Nothing in between the tires
  8. Read Schedule 1

8.2 - Check Tire Tread & Sidewall

Inspection Steps:

  • Outer sidewall:
    • No cuts, damage, or exposed cords
  • Tread surface:
    • Good condition
    • No cuts, damage, or exposed cords
  • Tread depth:
    • At least 3 mm on steer (front) tires
    • At least 1.5 mm on rear/trailer tires
  • Inner sidewall:
    • No cuts, damage, or exposed cords
    • Not touching any vehicle components
  • Dual tires:
    • Nothing stuck between them
    • Tires not touching each other
    • Not touching any vehicle component except mud flap
  • Markings:
    • No tire marked "Not for Highway Use"
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

9️⃣ Wheels, Hubs, and Fasteners

9.1 - Check Hub

Inspection Steps:

  • Sight glass:
    • Not broken
    • Hub oil level is maximum
  • Hub cap:
    • No cracks or damage
    • Cap fasteners are tight and secure
    • No oil leak
  • Inside wheel seal:
    • No leaks
    • No oil dripping on floor
  • No evidence of imminent wheel, hub, and bearing failure
  • Read Schedule 1

9.2 - Check Wheel Fasteners

Inspection Steps:

  • Wheel fasteners:
    • Present, tight, and secure
    • No missing fasteners
    • No crack or damage (check inside wheel)
  • All parts are tight and secure
  • No missing fasteners
  • No evidence of imminent wheel, hub, and bearing failure
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

9.3 - Check Wheel & Attaching Parts

Inspection Steps:

  • Pull the rim - should be tight, safe, and secure
  • All around the RIM:
    • No cracks, damage, or broken areas
    • All RIM fasteners are tight and secure
  • Inside the rim:
    • Attaching parts and fasteners are tight and secure
  • No evidence of imminent wheel, hub, and bearing failure
  • Read Schedule 1 (Minor and Major)

🔟 In-Cab Checks & Emergency Equipment

Note: During the in-cab check, you are not required to read the minor or major defects.

10 - In-Cab Inspection Procedure

Starting Up:

  1. Start the engine
  2. Check all lights are working when you turn the ignition on
  3. No major warning signs on dashboard (only parking brake and seat belt signs)

Gauges:

  • All gauges are working
  • Both air pressure gauges working properly
  • Pressure is at 100 PSI (normal operating range)

Steering Wheel:

  • Excessive free play less than 10 degrees
  • Works properly to the left and right
  • Properly attached to the tractor

Seat & Seat Belt:

  • Driver seat adjusts up/down and forward/reverse
  • Seat stays in locked position
  • Properly attached to the tractor
  • Seat belt:
    • No cuts or damages
    • Locks and unlocks properly
    • Tether securely attached on both sides

Glasses & Mirrors:

  • Both side window glasses:
    • No cracks or damages
    • No obstruction, clear view
  • Windshield:
    • No cracks or damages
    • Clear view, no obstruction
  • All rearview mirrors:
    • No cracks or damages
    • Clear view, properly adjusted

Wiper & Washer:

  • Wiper blade:
    • No damages
  • Wiper and washer working at all speeds
  • Cleaning windshield properly
  • Giving clear view

Heater & Defroster:

  • Heater and defroster working at all speeds
  • Face and leg mode working
  • Cleaning windshield properly
  • Giving clear view

10.1 - Emergency Equipment and Safety Devices

Fire Extinguisher:

  • Located on left side
  • Full
  • Safety pin is present
  • Use for minor fires

First-Aid Kit:

  • Present and contains:
    • Mask

1️⃣1️⃣ Uncoupling & Coupling Procedures

Uncoupling Procedure

Safety Preparations:

  • Apply parking brakes
  • Chock wheels to prevent vehicle movement
  • Ensure floor beneath landing gear is hard and even

Uncoupling Steps:

  1. Lower landing legs of trailer
  2. Ensure handle is secure
  3. Disconnect supply lines from trailer
  4. Secure supply lines to avoid damages
  5. Make sure king pin lock handle is OUT
  6. Jaw lock is OPEN
  7. Release tractor parking brakes (YELLOW IN)
  8. Do not release trailer parking brakes
  9. Pull tractor forward until fifth wheel is completely out (90 degree)
  10. Confirm landing legs are holding trailer weight
  11. Verify trailer is level

Inspection Before Coupling

Truck Inspection Steps:

  • Glad hands and electrical plug:
    • No cracks or damages
    • O-rings in good condition
    • Lines attached correctly
  • King pin lock handle:
    • OUT and not bent
  • Mounting plate:
    • No cracks or damages
  • All bolts:
    • Tight and secure
    • No missing fasteners
  • Fifth wheel:
    • No crack or damage
    • Tilting back
    • Has enough grease
    • Safety pin is present
  • Jaw lock is OPEN
  • Four-way flashers working
  • Check other side mounting plate and fastener

Coupling Procedure

Coupling Steps:

  1. Verify way is clear
  2. Honk horn twice to signal start of coupling
  3. Align tractor and trailer
  4. Touch fifth wheel and trailer to ensure proper connection

Trailer Checks After Coupling

Post-Coupling Inspection:

  1. Trailer height is good
  2. Inspect glad hands, electrical plug, and supply lines:
    • No damages
    • Properly attached
  3. Apron plate and king pin:
    • No cracks or damages
    • Has enough grease
  4. King pin and fifth wheel aligned correctly
  5. Conduct tug test to ensure trailer is securely connected
  6. Kingpin lock handle fully IN
  7. No gap between trailer and fifth wheel (both sides)
  8. Jaw is locked
  9. Connect supply lines to trailer

Additional Checks:

  1. Four-way flashers working correctly
  2. Landing legs UP and handle secure
  3. Inform examiner you will supply air and check for leaks
  4. Turn off engine and push RED VALVE IN
  5. Turn ignition on and listen for air leaks in supply line coupler
  6. Confirm no air leak in supply line coupler
  7. Push brake pedal (BLUE) and listen for leaks in service line coupler
  8. Confirm no air leak in service line coupler
  9. Pull out trailer parking brake (RED VALVE IN)
  10. Report: "Uncoupling and coupling is done"

⚙️ Brake Chamber Sizes

Common Brake Chamber Sizes

Standard Sizes:

  • Most common: Size 30
  • Other sizes: 16, 20, 24, and 36
  • Important: Size affects braking force — larger chambers apply more force

Standard vs. Long-Stroke (LS) Brake Chambers

Key Points:

  • Long-stroke chambers give more pushrod travel
  • Can improve braking but must still be inspected correctly

Identification:

  • If connection port is ROUND: Short or regular stroke chamber
  • If connection port is SQUARE: Long-stroke (LS) brake chamber
